# 14 CFR Part 23 Summary

## Why This Part Matters

Part 23 is the core technical authority for normal-category airplane certification. For many small-airplane and STC programs, this is the primary airworthiness rule set that drives the certification basis, means of compliance strategy, and the structure of substantiation reports.

## Practical Role In This Repo

- Treat Part 23 as a technical backbone, not just a reference list.
- Build project deliverables around the applicable Part 23 requirement sets and accepted means of compliance.
- Pair it with Part 21 for changed-product and STC programs.
- Use the FAA small-airplane policy portal to locate the accepted standards, policy material, and ACs that operationalize the rule text.

## High-Value Themes

- Performance-based structure: the rule is intentionally written around safety objectives rather than only prescriptive design details.
- Means of compliance discipline: the key downstream question is often not only what Part 23 section applies, but what accepted means of compliance or equivalent method will be used.
- Integrated system view: performance, handling, structures, propulsion, systems, occupant protection, and ICAs are tied together more tightly than many legacy checklists imply.
- Continued airworthiness matters early: ICA and airworthiness limitations should be developed as part of certification planning, not left as an afterthought.

## Sections That Usually Matter First

- Subpart B: flight and performance basis.
- Subpart C: structure basis, including loads and strength.
- Subpart D: design and construction topics, including systems and human factors.
- Subpart E: powerplant topics.
- Subpart F: equipment and operating information.
- ICA and airworthiness limitations sections tied to continued airworthiness obligations.

## Working Interpretation

For a Part 23 project, the most useful reporting pattern is usually requirement cluster -> selected means of compliance -> project evidence -> finding. A report organized only around drawings or only around tests tends to lose certification traceability.

## Source Boundary

This summary is an internal normalization of the current eCFR Part 23 text and related FAA policy posture. It is not a substitute for the regulation itself.
