# SMARTWING™ Sales Follow-Up Sequence

## The Problem This Solves

Most deals aren't lost to a "no." They're lost to silence after the first conversation. This sequence ensures every prospect gets consistent, value-driven follow-up that keeps the conversation alive without being pushy.

---

## Sequence Overview

| Day | Action | Content | Channel |
|-----|--------|---------|---------|
| 0 | First contact | Cold call / intro | Phone |
| 0 | Post-call follow-up | Personalized email + model one-pager | Email |
| 3 | Value touch #1 | FlightAware screenshot or customer story | Email |
| 7 | Second call attempt | Reference email content, advance conversation | Phone |
| 7 | Voicemail follow-up | Send brief email if voicemail left | Email |
| 14 | Value touch #2 | ROI calculation or ForeFlight angle | Email |
| 21 | Third call attempt | New angle based on persona | Phone |
| 30 | Value touch #3 | Sustainability or ActiveCare content | Email |
| 45 | Re-engagement | New content or event invitation | Email |
| 60 | Final active attempt | Direct ask — schedule or pause | Phone |
| 90+ | Nurture | Quarterly check-in | Email |

## Detailed Sequence

### Day 0 — Post-Call Email (send within 1 hour of first call)

**Subject:** [Model] Performance Data — As Discussed

> Hi [Name],
>
> Great speaking with you today. As promised, here's the SMARTWING™ performance comparison for the [Model]:
>
> [Attach model-specific one-pager]
>
> The key numbers for your aircraft:
> - Range: [stock] → [SMARTWING™] (+[delta] nm)
> - Fuel burn: [stock] → [SMARTWING™] ([X]% savings)
> - Vref resale value: 100% at installation
>
> I'd love to walk through the numbers specific to your flying profile. Would [day] or [day] work for a 15-minute call?
>
> [Rep Name]
> Tamarack Aerospace
> [Phone]

**CRM Action:** Log call outcome, persona assessment, key objections heard, next step date.

>

### Day 21 — Third Call Attempt (New Angle)

Shift the approach based on what you've learned:

| If they... | Try this angle... |
|------------|-------------------|
| Engaged with ROI email | "Let's refine the numbers with your actual hours and routes" |
| Engaged with FlightAware | "Would you like to see a demo flight?" |
| Haven't engaged at all | "I want to make sure I'm sending you relevant information. What matters most to you — range, fuel costs, resale value, or something else?" |
| Mentioned selling aircraft | "SMARTWING™ increases resale value. Vref data shows 100% ROI at installation. It sells faster and for more." |

### Day 30 — Value Touch #3

**Subject:** [Choose based on what hasn't been sent yet]

Options:

### Day 45 — Re-engagement

**Subject:** New [content type] for [Model] Owners

Send something genuinely new:
- A recently completed installation of the same model
- New FlightAware data from the fleet
- Upcoming trade show or event where they could see the aircraft
- CJP event invitation
- Pilot Proficiency Course (FAA WINGS) — "We offer a pilot proficiency course you might find valuable, regardless of whether you're considering SMARTWING™."

---

### Day 60 — Final Active Attempt

**Call script:**

> "Hi [Name], it's [Rep] with Tamarack. I've sent you several pieces of information on SMARTWING™ for your [Model] over the past couple months. I want to be respectful of your time — is this something you'd like to continue exploring, or should I check back at a later date?"

**Three possible outcomes:**
1. **Ready to advance:** Schedule a detailed call or demo flight
2. **Not now, but later:** "When would be a good time to reconnect?" Set the date, move to nurture
3. **Not interested:** "I appreciate your time. If anything changes, I'm here." Remove from active sequence, add to annual check-in

---

### Day 90+ — Nurture (Quarterly)

- Brief, low-pressure email with one piece of new content
- Holiday or seasonal check-in
- Event invitations
- Fleet milestone announcements (e.g., "250th installation completed")
- New model certifications or product updates

## Rules of the Sequence

1. **Never send a spec sheet without a scheduled follow-up.** Every document you send should have a reason to call about it.

2. **Log everything in CRM.** Every call, email, voicemail, and objection. Your future self (and your manager) will thank you.

3. **Personalize every touch.** Use their name, their model, their airports, their mission. Generic emails get deleted.

4. **Match content to persona.** Go-Getters want ROI. Old Pros want data. Skeptics want proof. Collectors want exclusivity.

5. **Respect the "no."** A clear "not interested" is better than chasing silence. Move them to nurture and revisit in 6–12 months.

6. **The best follow-up is a referral.** If a current customer flies the same model, ask if they'd be willing to take a call from the prospect. Peer validation closes deals.
