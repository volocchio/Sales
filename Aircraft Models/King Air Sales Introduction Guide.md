# Performance SMARTWING™ for King Air 200 & 350 — Sales Introduction Guide

## A New Market: Turboprop Operators

Tamarack's Performance SMARTWING™ technology extends the proven ATLAS load-alleviation platform to the Beechcraft King Air family. This opens an entirely new customer base beyond the Citation/M2 market.

---

## King Air 200

**Website tagline:** Extend missions, improve performance & go further.

### Target Customers
- Part 91 owner-operators (corporate flight departments)
- Part 135 charter operators
- Special mission operators (air ambulance, survey, ISR)
- Government / military operators
- Regional air carriers

### Why King Air 200 Operators Should Care

The King Air 200 is one of the most versatile turboprops ever built, but it has inherent aerodynamic limitations:
- Fuel efficiency decreases at higher altitudes where it should be most efficient
- Range is limited for long overwater or remote operations
- Payload/range trade-offs force difficult operational decisions
- Older aircraft are competing against newer turboprop designs

**SMARTWING™ addresses every one of these by increasing aspect ratio and managing wing loads — the same proven physics as the Citation STC, adapted for the King Air platform.**

### Prospecting Approach

**Different buyer profile than Citation market:**
- King Air operators are often mission-driven (medevac, cargo, survey) rather than personal/business travel
- ROI for commercial operators is measured in revenue per flight hour, not personal fuel savings
- Fleet operators think in terms of per-aircraft economics across 10–50 airframes
- Government/military buyers have procurement processes, not impulse decisions

**SPIN adaptation for King Air:**
- **Situation:** "What missions do you fly? What's your average stage length? How many hours per year per airframe?"
- **Problem:** "Do you ever have to reduce payload for range? Turn down missions that are just outside your range?"
- **Implication:** "What does a turned-down mission cost you in revenue? What does an extra fuel stop cost in crew time and scheduling?"
- **Need-Payoff:** "If your 200s could carry more weight farther on less fuel, how would that change your dispatch decisions?"

## Key Differences: King Air Sales vs. Citation Sales

| Factor | Citation Market | King Air Market |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Buyer type | Owner-pilot / personal | Operator / fleet manager |
| Decision driver | Personal ROI / lifestyle | Mission capability / operating economics |
| Purchase process | Individual decision | Committee / procurement |
| Quantity | Single aircraft | Fleet potential (5–50) |
| Cycle length | 2–6 months | 6–18 months |
| Key metric | Fuel savings + Vref | Revenue per flight hour + dispatch rate |
| Emotional driver | Performance / aesthetics | Mission success / competitive advantage |

### Adapting the Sales Approach

1. **Lead with mission capability, not personal benefit.** These aren't owner-pilots flying for fun — they're operators running a business on these aircraft.

2. **Speak in fleet economics.** "If you have 12 King Air 200s flying 600 hours each, the fleet-wide fuel savings from SMARTWING™ are $[X] per year."

3. **Address maintenance concerns differently.** Turboprop operators are highly maintenance-conscious. Emphasize the simplicity of the ATLAS system and ActiveCare Warranty.

4. **Understand the procurement process.** Government and military sales require different documentation, evaluation criteria, and approval chains. Be prepared for a longer cycle.

5. **Special mission angle:** For medevac operators: "SMARTWING™ means you can accept patient transfers at greater distances without refueling. That's not an economic argument — it's a patient outcome argument."

## Competitive Landscape — King Air Modifications

The King Air aftermarket is mature and competitive. Operators are familiar with modifications. Key competitors/alternatives:
- Raisbeck Engineering (various King Air mods)
- Blackhawk Aerospace (engine upgrades)
- Various passive winglet/tip tank providers
- Trading up to King Air 360/360ER

**Positioning:** SMARTWING™ is complementary to engine upgrades (both reduce operating costs). It's superior to passive winglets for the same reasons as in the Citation market. And it's far more cost-effective than trading up to a new airframe.
